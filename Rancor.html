Rancor Simulator

At the University of Toronto, I led the expert analysis and optimization of the Rancor Nuclear Power Plant Microworld Simulator, collaborating with Idaho National Laboratories to design test scenarios, suggest interface improvements, and implement setup modifications to support future research objectives for graduate students. As the primary Rancor expert at UofT, I also developed a Python-based data analysis tool that processes simulator output files, cleans and compiles data into precise success metrics, enabling graduate students to perform detailed scenario analysis using real-time data, significantly improving research accuracy beyond traditional participant surveys. Additionally, I directed the first pilot testing of the Rancor Microworld Power Plant Simulator at UofT, replicating a previous study with the new tool, which allowed for participant feedback and the identification of improvements in training materials, software, and experimental setup for future research. I compiled a comprehensive pilot testing report and an error-fix log, providing future researchers with a valuable reference to troubleshoot issues efficiently, saving time and streamlining the research process.
